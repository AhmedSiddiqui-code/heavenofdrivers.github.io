LOTUS EVORA

Overview

The Lotus Evora GT is the British automaker's lone American model, but this lightweight sports car has exotic elements and fantastic handling. The 2020 Evora GT actually replaces the previous 400 moniker, but the latest mid-engined Lotus has mainly mild updates.

Engine, Ride, and Handling

Every Evora GT features a supercharged 3.5-liter V-6 that makes 416 horsepower and between 317 and 332 lb-ft of torque. That latter number is produced when the engine pairs with the six-speed automatic transmission, while the former is associated with the six-speed manual. All that power is funneled to the rear wheels, with the manual gearbox adding a limited-slip differential for improved traction. Although we haven't driven the 2020 Evora GT, we have slipped behind the wheel of a 2017 Evora 400. The most recent version is even lighter and faster, with a curb weight of 3104 pounds and an estimated top speed of 188 mph. Lotus also provides the GT with extra downforce and standard carbon-fiber body panels. Likewise, performance buffs can cut even more weight with the optional Carbon Pack. The Evora GT rides on 19-inch wheels up front and 20s out back that are wrapped with a set of super-sticky Michelin Pilot Sport Cup 2 tires.

Interior and Technology

The Evora GT can hold between two and four occupants, depending on which configuration is chosen. The cabin is covered in faux suede and features standard Sparco race seats. The dashboard design isn't cutting-edge, but it mixes physical switchgear with fancier elements such as a 7.0-inch infotainment screen that supports Apple CarPlay and Android Auto capability. While the cockpit prioritizes the driver—as every true sports car should—the Evora's innards can be customized via Lotus's exclusive program. This allows owners to select preferred paint colors and special leather upholstery.
