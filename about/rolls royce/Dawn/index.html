DAWN 2020

OVERVIEW

Luxurious, exclusive, exquisite: take your pick of adjectives for the lovely Dawn. Basically a Wraith coupe under the skin, the Dawn has sheetmetal that curves and swoops, making it, according to Rolls (but we don't necessarily disagree) the "sexiest Rolls-Royce ever built."

Engines, Ride, and Handling

Under the hood is a 6.6-liter twin-turbocharged V-12 that makes 563 horsepower, so the Dawn is as fleet as it is sexy. At our test track, the Dawn rushed to 60 mph in just 4.3 seconds. It moves gracefully, albeit deliberately, and cossets its occupants when driving over even the harshest potholed roads. Even the eight-speed automatic transmission is tuned for tranquility; it uses GPS to predict when and where it should change gears based on whether the car is going uphill or downhill or going around corners.

Interior and Technology

Rear-hinged doors provide access to the Dawn's cabin and inside, there is room for four adult passengers; occupants will be dazzled by the gorgeous butter-soft leather and acres of real wood trim. Front-seats can be optioned with a massaging function while thick, lambs wool floormats pamper the tootsies of the lucky occupants. But the Dawn isn't just about old-school luxury, it's also a technological powerhouse; a suite of driver-assistance features is offered and includes a novel night-vision feature to help drivers avoid striking animals or pedestrians when driving after dark. Touchscreen infotainment is standard but Rolls-Royce offers a TV tuner as an option so that passengers can watch their favorite programs while on the go.
